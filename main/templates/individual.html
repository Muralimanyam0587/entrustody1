What is an Asset Custodian?

Advisors are not allowed to possess the assets that they purchase for your account. Instead, an asset custodian is responsible for holding and safekeeping your assets. These firms typically also handle processing of transactions, collecting and distributing dividends and interest, and generating account statements. Advisors manage your assets through the software platform provided by that custodian. Financial assets are bought and sold on the custodian's "supermarket‚Äù.

Your advisor's custodian may not be aligned with your best interest.

The custodial industry is highly concentrated, with 80% of all custodied assets held at the Big 3 firms. As a result, there is little incentive to change or innovate, and the interests of the major custodians have become misaligned with those of advisors and investors. Some ways that custodians may be constraining your financial growth:

Hidden Fees

The major custodians charge fees to asset managers of financial products, such as mutual funds, that want to appear on their supermarkets. The managers cover this cost by marking up the price you pay for their product; this mark-up is rarely made clear to investors.

Product Favoritism

Some custodians offer their in-house financial products alongside the other products in their supermarkets. Custodians may make their own products more cost-competitive, but they may not be the best fit for your portfolio.

Outdated Platforms

Custodian software platforms tend to have limited functionality and often require your advisor to invest in additional technology, which can result in higher fees for you. Advisors have to allocate time to managing and integrating the various software reports- time that could be better spent working with investors.
